\begin{center}
    \bfseries \zihao{3} 摘~要
\end{center}

针对传统检索增强生成（Retrieval-Augmented Generation, RAG）模型在复杂多跳问答中面临的证据碎片化与生成幻觉问题，本文实现并分析了一套基于置信度重检与后验验证的RAG框架原型。该框架将RAG视为一个兼顾正确率、成本与安全性的序列决策过程，重点围绕基于重排序置信度的伪相关反馈策略（Confidence-based Pseudo-Relevance Feedback）与基于 CoVe 思想的启发式后验验证机制展开。

在实现上，系统首先采用序列化对齐策略，将表格与图谱片段转化为统一格式参与检索；随后以重排序得分作为置信度指标，用启发式阈值控制二次检索触发；最后在生成后执行基于 CoVe 思想的声明核验近似，当发现声明与证据冲突或证据缺失时返回“无答案”（No-Answer）。

本文共组织了四组实验：系统级全量消融、更严格基线与跨数据集对比、阈值敏感性与部署代价分析，以及误差分布与验证器诊断。结果表明，纯文本基线在 HotpotQA 全量测试上的有效 F1 为 6.16，而异构序列化会将其拉低至 4.59；纯文本设置中加入 Adaptive 控制组（A2）几乎不改变最终 F1，仅带来轻微的证据覆盖提升；加入严格验证的全功能系统（D）则出现 97.2\% 的极端拒答率，使有效 F1 降至 0.06。分桶统计显示，Adaptive 的主要收益集中在 bridge 类问题上，其 SupportAllHit@5 相对 Dense + Rerank 提升 7.92 个百分点，而在 comparison 类问题上的提升仅为 1.04 个百分点。验证器对比进一步说明，软阈值验证几乎不拒答但保留了较高的不安全接受率，严格阈值则带来了近乎不可用的错误拒答。总体而言，本文的主要贡献不在于证明“全功能系统已经足够鲁棒”，而在于通过可复现实验揭示 RAG 系统在证据覆盖、部署成本与安全约束之间必须显式权衡的关键矛盾，并为后续基于反馈闭环与软置信度判别的优化提供了明确方向。

\begin{center}
    \bfseries \zihao{3} Abstract
\end{center}

To address the challenges of evidence fragmentation and generation hallucination in traditional Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) models, this thesis implements and analyzes a RAG framework prototype based on confidence-aware re-retrieval and post-hoc verification. By formulating RAG as a sequential decision process that balances accuracy, cost, and safety, the framework focuses on two core mechanisms: confidence-based Pseudo-Relevance Feedback (PRF) and Verification-constrained Generation based on Chain-of-Verification (CoVe).

First, in the data ingestion phase, the system employs a serialization alignment strategy to unify tables and knowledge graph snippets for retrieval. Second, in the retrieval decision phase, the system uses reranking scores as a confidence metric and adopts a heuristic threshold to trigger secondary retrieval, thereby striking a balance between multi-hop evidence coverage and computational latency. Finally, in the generation phase, the system performs fact-checking verification; when conflicts or knowledge gaps are detected between the generated claims and the underlying evidence, the system triggers a safe "No-Answer" rejection mechanism. 

Comprehensive experiments reveal that the plain-text baseline reaches an effective F1 of 6.16 on the full HotpotQA evaluation, whereas heterogeneous serialization reduces it to 4.59. Adding Adaptive retrieval in the text-only control group barely changes end-to-end F1, though it improves evidence coverage slightly; applying strict CoVe in the full system yields a 97.2\% rejection rate and drives effective F1 down to 0.06. Additional bucket analyses show that Adaptive retrieval is mainly beneficial for bridge questions, while verifier comparison shows a stark trade-off: soft CoVe keeps unsafe acceptance high, whereas strict CoVe sharply increases false rejections. These findings highlight the risks of naive module stacking in RAG systems and underscore the tension among effectiveness, cost, and reliability that must be explicitly balanced.
